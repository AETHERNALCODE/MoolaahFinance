### I. Abstract: Exploring the Biological-to-Digital Frontier
This report summarizes the 365-day results of an experimental deployment (Pool 10032). The objective was to test the viability of the **OASIS Stochastic Model**—a physiological tracking system built to bridge real-world biological growth with on-chain financial logic. 

Moolaah was built from a place of curiosity: *Can we create a transparent, code-based system that reflects the complex reality of livestock growth?* This audit suggests the answer is yes, with some fascinating technical outcomes.

---

### II. The "OASIS" Engine: A Study in Resilience
The **OASIS Variant** was our technical response to environmental volatility. Rather than optimizing for raw speed, we engineered the logic to prioritize **Systemic Resilience**.
*   **Biological Logic**: The code simulates energy redirection. In high-stress windows, the system prioritizes metabolic stability.
*   **Findings**: During the initial 180 days, we observed zero births. While counter-intuitive, this was an **accurate biological success**; the heifers were maturing according to the 300kg threshold set in the code, showing the model correctly respects biological constraints over "infinite growth" fantasies.

---

### III. Protocol Architecture & Operational Safety
We designed the liquidity structure to ensure the experiment was "fail-safe":
1.  **Asset Buffer**: We maintained a physical reserve to ensure that if an animal was lost (mortality), the on-chain metadata could be kept accurate via an asset substitution. This keeps the "digital twin" logic intact.
2.  **Reserve Strategy**: Roughly 40% of the initial capital was held in a maintenance reserve. This ensures that no matter what the market does, the physical needs of the herd (feed, health) are always covered by the code’s treasury.

### IV. Maturation & Reproductive Cycles
The most exciting technical milestone occurred around Day 240.
*   **Maturity Event**: As the average herd weight crossed the **300kg graduation mark**, the reproductive logic "unlocked."
*   **Result**: 27 births occurred in the final quarter. In the context of the protocol, these births act as an "in-kind" growth of the pool, increasing the asset count through biological compounding rather than new capital.
